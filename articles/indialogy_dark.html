Indialogy

By Mirwais Balkhi · June 2024

In the southwest of Delhi, on the western side of the Shamsi pond, in the middle of a narrow alley, there is an old mosque on the right hand side, in the courtyard of which is the tomb of Sheikh Abu al-Majd Abdul Haq Muhaddith Dehlavi, "known as Muhaddith Dehlavi". This Sheikh of Hadith is named after the people of Bukhara, from which his father Sheikh Saifuddin migrated to India. Muhaddith Dehlavi learned Islamic sciences (rational and traditional) at the age of 20 or 22. He also studied Hadith from Arab scholars for several years during his Hajj journey.
